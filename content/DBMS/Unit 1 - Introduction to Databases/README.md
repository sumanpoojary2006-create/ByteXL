## How each lesson is written

Each lesson follows the house style established in the Python course: a standardized **Introduction** heading (no page-title H1), a story-led flow with real-world examples and situation-based questions under natural headings, and a closing **Conclusion**. No emojis, no em dashes.

This unit is entirely conceptual: no SQL syntax appears anywhere in it. That is deliberate. Every idea here (data vs. information, files vs. databases, what a DBMS actually is) needs to be solid before a single `CREATE TABLE` shows up in Unit 3, and nothing in this unit assumes SQL either, so it can be read by someone who has never touched a database client.

Lessons follow a single recurring character, Devika, the librarian at a college library: a card-catalogue and spreadsheet system that quietly falls apart under real use, the vocabulary to name what a database and a DBMS actually are, a tour of databases hiding behind everyday apps, a look at the different shapes data can take, a peek inside the DBMS itself, the different people who rely on it daily, and finally why this course, like most of the industry, starts with the relational model.
